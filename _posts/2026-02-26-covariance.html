Covariance

Covariance is a statistical measure of the joint variability of two random variables. It indicates whether two variables move in the same or opposite directions.

It can be

  • positive: indicating that two variables change together
  • negative: indicating they change in opposite directions
  • or zero: indicating absolute independence from each other

Formula: $cov(X,Y)=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}{(X_i-\bar{X})(Y_i-\bar{Y})}}{n-1}$

For populations n-1 in the denominator changes to the whole population size N.

Calculate Covariance in Python

In[40]:
from statistics import covariance
covariance([1,2,3], [3,2,1])

In[41]:
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from statistics import covariance

def plot(x,y):
    cov_xy = covariance(x,y)
    plt.scatter(x,y)
    plt.title(f"cov(x,y)={cov_xy:.3f}")
    plt.xlabel("X")
    plt.ylabel("Y")

# positive linear correlation
x = np.linspace(1,10,10)
y = np.linspace(10,100,10)

plt.figure(figsize=(12,5))
plt.subplot(1,2,1)
plot(x,y)

# negative linear correlation
x = np.linspace(1,10,10)
y = np.linspace(10,100,10)[::-1]

plt.subplot(1,2,2)
plot(x,y)

plt.show()

Covariance vs Correlation

Both assess the direction of the relationship between two variables. The difference is that correlation also assesses its stength and is a standardized measure, compareable across data sets.

Interpreting Covariance

Covariance can be tough to inperpret as its formula allows values to range from negative to positive infinity and it is strongly effected by the magnitude and scale of the variables. A high or low covariance doesn't imply strong or weak correlation. Covariance doesn't give reliable information about the stength of the relationship between variables, just about the direction. To assess its stength use the correlation coefficient instead, which standardizes covariance to a value between -1 and 1.

Covariance matrix

The covariance matrix, also known as variance-covariance matrix, is a square matrix that represents the variance of each element along the major diagonal, and the covariance among the non-diagonal elements.

$\begin{bmatrix}var(X) & cov(X, Y) \\ cov(Y, X) & var(Y)\end{bmatrix}$
